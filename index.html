Selected Projects

Foveated Rendering in AR

Graduate-level Coursework in Advanced Interactive Technologies

Augmented reality (AR) is to augment virtual information into real world. However, too many virtual information may make user feel dizzy, especially in some real scenes that are complex. Thus, we apply foveated rendering which provides less detail in peripheral region on augmented reality devices. We expect that it may not only reduce the complexity of view but also potentially improve the performance of rendering.

An UAV Autopilot System for Sports Player Tracking

Undergraduate Project, Conference of Taiwan Human-Computer Interaction (TAICHI) 2017

We proposed an UAV (Unmanned Aerial Vehicle) autopilot system based on character recognition, human detection and human tracking technologies to track sports player automatically. It reduces human labor to decrease the cost, providing a safer environment for players to compete and a better view angle for broadcast. Experiments have shown the proposed system can track human well and is stable even when losing sports players number or facing pedestrian disturbance.
